About

It is not about us, it is about technology that brings tangible benefits for investors and environment.

We are a Polish family-owned company, that provides economically reliable solutions for plastic waste recycling, based on over 25 years of experience in pyrolytic conversion of plastic waste into fuel oils.

POLAND

In the 90’s of the last century, thanks to the pyrolytic oil excise tax waiver, Poland became the nation the most dynamically developing pyrolysis of the plastic waste, also known as plastic-waste-to-fuel technology. More than 20 industrial size pyrolytic reactors have been built at the country the size of California.

In 2005 pyrolytic fuel and crude oil fuel taxes have been aligned and pyrolytic oil production was financially viable only with the use of the biggest and constantly operated technologies.

However, with growing plastic production, its still remains the most efficient technology capable to cover demand for plastic waste conversion equal to its global production.

EUROPE

In January 2018, the European Union released its Strategy for Plastics in a Circular Economy. One of its objectives is to increase recycling of plastic and reuse of plastic packaging by 2030 to 55%.

By the use of low temperature pyrolysis, high temperature pyrolysis or extremely high temperature molecular reactors, this target is achievable or even beatable, because parallelly it's able to accept dumped and previously recycled plastics to commence process of environment decontamination.

NORTH AMERICA

In the U.S. and Canada, current recycling infrastructure recovers less than 10% of post-consumer plastics, and today’s supply of recycled plastics meets just 6% of real demand. Poorly managed, plastic wastes fill the environment.

Actual size of globally dumped plastic equals size of Mt. Everest. If current trends continue, global demand for plastics is forecasted to triple by 2050. At the same time, there may be more plastic than fish in oceans by 2050.

To reverse the current trend and address the global market demands, thermal conversion that repetitively keeps plastics in play is needed at scale (i.e. in 2018, nearly 42 million metric tons of plastic were produced in North America).

Process

Rapid development of technology in the last decade of the 20th century was strong enough to establish the mature pyrolysis of plastic waste, that lets investors obtain the fuel oils, synthetic diesel and other petrochemical products in commercial scale.

Our process is continuous, automated and able to accept contaminated, mixed streams of plastics. Close loop of energy. Source of renewable energy for the process itself. We help investors to take advantage from zero emission of the pyrolysis and lower their carbon footprint. Fuels created by our systems have a 75% lower carbon footprint compared to traditional fossil fuels.
